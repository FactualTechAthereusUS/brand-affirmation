## Compounded vs. FDA-Approved Medications

Physicians on the Blissley platform may prescribe compounded medications prepared by independent, state-licensed 503A compounding pharmacies. It is important that you understand the distinction between compounded and FDA-approved medications.

**FDA-approved medications** such as Ozempic®, Wegovy®, Mounjaro®, and Zepbound® have undergone rigorous review by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration for safety, efficacy, and quality. They are manufactured under controlled conditions and subject to ongoing regulatory oversight.

**Compounded medications** are custom-prepared by licensed pharmacies for specific patients based on a physician's prescription. Compounded medications:

- Are NOT FDA-approved
- Have NOT been evaluated by the FDA for safety, effectiveness, or quality
- Do not undergo the same clinical testing as FDA-approved drugs
- May contain the same active ingredients as FDA-approved drugs but may differ in formulation, concentration, or inactive ingredients
- Should not be prescribed when an FDA-approved alternative is clinically appropriate and available

You should only receive a compounded medication if your physician determines it is medically necessary for your specific situation.

Your physician will obtain your informed consent before prescribing any compounded medication.

## GLP-1 Medications: Educational Overview

This section provides general educational information about GLP-1 receptor agonist medications. This information is not a substitute for the clinical guidance of your physician.

### What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonists are a class of medications that work by mimicking the GLP-1 hormone produced naturally by the body. GLP-1 helps regulate appetite, blood sugar, and feelings of fullness. Some medications also target the GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptor, which affects additional aspects of metabolism.

When used as part of a comprehensive weight management program that may include dietary changes and physical activity, these medications may support meaningful and sustained weight loss. Clinical trials of FDA-approved semaglutide and tirzepatide have demonstrated average weight reduction of 15 to 22 percent of body weight over 68 weeks.

Individual results vary. Weight management is influenced by many factors including consistency with treatment, dietary habits, physical activity, sleep, stress, and individual biological differences.

### Medical History You Must Disclose to Your Physician

Tell your physician about your full medical history before beginning treatment. It is especially important to disclose if you have or have had:

- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- Pancreatitis (inflammation of the pancreas)
- Gallbladder disease or gallstones
- Kidney disease
- Diabetic retinopathy
- Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes
- Heart disease or history of cardiovascular events
- Gastrointestinal disorders or gastroparesis
- History of eating disorders
- Depression, anxiety, or suicidal ideation
- Any current medications, including insulin or other diabetes medications

**Inform your physician if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.** GLP-1 medications are not recommended during pregnancy.

Providing incomplete or inaccurate medical history to obtain treatment may result in harm, including serious injury or death. Withholding information does not affect liability.

### Common Side Effects

Studies have shown that GLP-1 and dual GLP-1/GIP medications may cause the following side effects. Not all patients experience these effects, and many resolve as the body adjusts to the medication:

- Nausea (most common, particularly in early weeks of treatment)
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Constipation
- Stomach pain or cramping
- Indigestion or heartburn
- Decreased appetite
- Fatigue
- Headache
- Dizziness
- Flatulence

### Serious Side Effects: Seek Immediate Medical Attention

Discontinue use and seek emergency medical care if you experience:

- Severe abdominal pain that may radiate to your back (possible pancreatitis)
- Persistent vomiting or inability to keep fluids down (risk of dehydration)
- Rapid heartbeat, shortness of breath, or chest pain
- Symptoms of a severe allergic reaction: rash, swelling of the face or throat, difficulty breathing
- Vision changes
- Signs of kidney problems: decreased urination, swelling in legs
- Symptoms of low blood sugar if you are diabetic: shakiness, sweating, confusion
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide; changes in mood or behavior

**Report any side effects to your Blissley physician immediately through your patient portal.**

### Dosing and Administration

Your physician will prescribe a starting dose and a titration schedule appropriate for your health profile. Dose increases are gradual to minimize side effects. Do not increase your dose without physician approval.

Injectable medications are administered subcutaneously (just under the skin) once weekly. Your medication ships with complete injection instructions and supplies. Contact your physician if you have any questions about administration.

Oral formulations may be available where prescribed. Follow your physician's specific instructions for the formulation prescribed to you.

### Drug Interactions

GLP-1 medications may affect the absorption of other oral medications. Tell your physician about all medications, supplements, and vitamins you take.

## Shipping and Storage

Your medication ships in temperature-controlled packaging. Upon receipt:

- Refrigerate immediately at 36 to 46 degrees Fahrenheit (2 to 8 degrees Celsius)
- Do not freeze
- Once a vial has been opened and punctured, use within the window specified on your medication label
- Store away from light
- Keep out of reach of children

Prescription medications do not come in child-resistant packaging unless you specifically request it. If you have children in your household, please request child-resistant packaging or store medication in a locked location.

---

## Disposal

Do not dispose of syringes or sharps in regular household trash. Use the sharps disposal container included in your supply kit. Local pharmacies and community programs often accept full sharps containers for disposal.
